Concrete Flatwork

Concrete flatwork refers to any type of concrete work on a flat surface. Think sidewalks, walkways, or any concrete surface that requires an even, smooth finish.

Concrete is a popular choice among homeowners because of its beauty and structural integrity. It’s relatively low maintenance so it will keep looking and functioning like new for years to come. It can withstand harsh wind weather conditions, which is a major plus in a cold climate like Denver.

Just like any concrete application, concrete flatwork requires skill, precision, the right equipment, and the right mixture of aggregates. Concrete will need to be properly mixed, poured, and cured to ensure a flawless final product, which is why it’s essential to work with a professional concrete service in Denver, CO.

Concrete Foundations

Concrete foundations commonly take three different forms: T-shaped, slab-on-grade, and frost protected. The traditional T-shaped foundation provides support to a structure where the ground usually freezes. Concrete footings are placed below the frost line and are wide enough to provide extra support to the foundation.

Because of Denver’s cold climate, slab-on-grade is typically not used here because of ground freezing. However, the slab-on-grade design can be used with a frost protected concrete slab. Insulation is used to retain heat and keep the footings above freezing. This method can also be accomplished with a single pour of concrete, compared to the three pours in a T-shaped foundation.

Arise Constructions can guide you on selecting the right concrete foundation for your project. Our methods are designed to match the cold temperatures and snowy, icy conditions in Denver and surrounding areas to produce an optimal outcome.
